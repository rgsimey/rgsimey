About

I'm Simon Ross-Gill, a graphic designer and community developer based on the Isle of Arran, where I grew up. My design work spans publication layout, brand identity, illustration, interpretive maps and web design — primarily for environmental, conservation and community organisations in Scotland and internationally.

Clients have included, in Scotland: NatureScot (prev. Scottish Natural Heritage), Community of Arran Seabed Trust (COAST), Voice for Arran, Arran Environment Festival, Woodside Arran CIC, Wee Mac Arran, Corrie's Capers, Cruickshanks Boutique B&B, the Whiting Bay & Districts Improvements Committee, and Love Millstone Point. International: Aracari Travel (Lima, Peru), Horizon Guides (Sheffield, England), White Tiger PR (London), West Cork Fit (Ireland), Kalpak Travel (Switzerland), Travel Without Plastic (Canary Isles), Group-Q (Denver, USA) and Infinite Safari Adventures (Los Angeles, USA).

Alongside design, I co-lead the Arran Pioneer Project and am currently Secretary of Dùthchas Arainn CBS, working on community-led affordable housing and woodland crofting. The natural landscape of Arran - its mounains and glens, woodland and coastline - is a constant influence on my creative work.

I use Affinity Designer and Publisher for print and vector work, for web design I am focussed on lightweight but impacting HTML templates for static sites. I work at transparent rates with a tiered pricing model to make quality design accessible to community and third-sector clients.
